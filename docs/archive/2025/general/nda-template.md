# Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) Template

**Purpose:** Standard NDA template for protecting confidential information shared with vendors, partners, investors, or potential collaborators.

---

## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**Agreement Date:** [DATE]

**This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement")** is entered into between:

**Disclosing Party:**
[COMPANY NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[CONTACT EMAIL]

**Receiving Party:**
[COMPANY/INDIVIDUAL NAME]
[ADDRESS]
[CONTACT EMAIL]

**1. PURPOSE**

The parties wish to engage in discussions regarding [PURPOSE OF DISCUSSION, e.g., potential business collaboration, investment opportunity, vendor evaluation] and may disclose confidential information to each other.

**2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION**

"Confidential Information" means all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by one party ("Disclosing Party") to the other party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, and financial information
- Product specifications, designs, and technical data
- Customer lists, marketing plans, and sales information
- Software, code, algorithms, and technology
- Proprietary methodologies and processes
- Any information marked as "Confidential" or "Proprietary"

**3. EXCLUSIONS**

Confidential Information does not include information that:

- Was publicly known at the time of disclosure
- Becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement
- Was rightfully known by Receiving Party before disclosure
- Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information
- Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction

**4. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY**

Receiving Party agrees to:

- Hold Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Use Confidential Information solely for the stated purpose
- Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent
- Take reasonable precautions to protect Confidential Information
- Not reverse engineer or attempt to derive proprietary information
- Return or destroy Confidential Information upon request

**5. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES**

Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:

- To employees, advisors, or consultants who need to know (and who are bound by confidentiality)
- As required by law or court order (with prior notice to Disclosing Party)
- With written consent from Disclosing Party

**6. TERM**

This Agreement shall remain in effect for [X YEARS] from the date of execution, or until terminated by either party with [X] days written notice.

**7. RETURN OF MATERIALS**

Upon termination or request, Receiving Party shall:

- Return all Confidential Information and copies
- Destroy any materials containing Confidential Information
- Provide written confirmation of return/destruction

**8. NO LICENSE**

This Agreement does not grant any license or rights to use Confidential Information except as stated herein.

**9. REMEDIES**

Receiving Party acknowledges that:

- Breach would cause irreparable harm
- Disclosing Party is entitled to injunctive relief
- Disclosing Party may seek monetary damages
- Receiving Party shall reimburse legal costs

**10. GENERAL PROVISIONS**

- **Governing Law:** [Province/Territory], Canada
- **Entire Agreement:** This Agreement supersedes all prior agreements
- **Modifications:** Must be in writing and signed by both parties
- **Severability:** If any provision is invalid, remainder remains in effect
- **Notices:** Must be in writing and sent to addresses above

## ONE-WAY NDA VARIATION

**For situations where only one party is disclosing:**

Use the same template but modify:

- Section 1: "Disclosing Party wishes to disclose..."
- Section 2: Only Disclosing Party's information is protected
- Section 4: Only Receiving Party has obligations

---

## NOTES FOR USE

**When to Use:**
- Before sharing sensitive business information
- When evaluating vendors/partners
- During investor discussions
- When exploring collaborations

**Customization:**
- Specify purpose clearly
- Set appropriate term (typically 2-5 years)
- Define Confidential Information scope
- Adjust for mutual vs. one-way disclosure

**Best Practices:**
- Execute before sharing any sensitive information
- Mark documents "Confidential" when sharing
- Keep records of what was disclosed
- Review periodically for relevance

**Legal Review:**
- Have a lawyer review before use
- Ensure compliance with applicable laws
- Consider jurisdiction-specific requirements
